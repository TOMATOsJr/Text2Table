PROJECT: PARAGRAPH → KNOWLEDGE GRAPH → TABLE USING OPEN INFORMATION EXTRACTION

Goal
The goal of this project is to convert a biography paragraph into a structured table. The system first constructs a knowledge graph from the paragraph using Open Information Extraction (OpenIE) and then converts that graph into a table format similar to a Wikipedia infobox.

Example Input Paragraph
Leonard Shenoff Randle (born February 12, 1949) is a former Major League Baseball player who played for the Seattle Mariners.

Desired Final Table

attribute | value
name | Leonard Shenoff Randle
birth_date | February 12 1949
occupation | baseball player
team | Seattle Mariners

STAGE 1: TEXT PREPROCESSING

Purpose
Prepare the paragraph so that information extraction can work correctly.

Tasks performed in this stage

1. Unicode normalization so all characters follow a consistent encoding.
2. Removal of dataset-specific tokens such as -lrb-, -rrb-, -lsb-, etc.
3. Normalize punctuation spacing (for example remove spaces before commas and periods).
4. Remove unnecessary extra whitespace.
5. Split the paragraph into sentences.

Example

Original text
Leonard Shenoff Randle -lrb- born February 12 , 1949 -rrb- is a former baseball player .

Cleaned text
Leonard Shenoff Randle (born February 12, 1949) is a former baseball player.

Sentence segmentation

Sentence 1
Leonard Shenoff Randle (born February 12, 1949) is a former baseball player.

Sentence 2
He played for the Seattle Mariners.

Why this step is important
OpenIE systems usually operate at the sentence level, so clean and properly segmented sentences are required for accurate extraction.

STAGE 2: OPEN INFORMATION EXTRACTION

Purpose
Automatically extract relational facts from each sentence without requiring a predefined schema.

Concept
OpenIE extracts tuples of the form

(subject, relation, object)

Example sentence
Randle played for the Seattle Mariners.

Extraction
(Randle, played for, Seattle Mariners)

Example sentence
Randle was born on February 12, 1949.

Extraction
(Randle, born on, February 12 1949)

Important property
Unlike traditional relation extraction systems, OpenIE does not require predefined relation types. The relation phrase is extracted exactly as it appears in the text.

Examples of relations extracted by OpenIE

played for
born on
worked at
served as
won

Output of this stage
A list of extracted tuples representing factual relationships from the paragraph.

Example extracted tuples

(Randle, born on, February 12 1949)
(Randle, is, baseball player)
(Randle, played for, Seattle Mariners)

STAGE 3: KNOWLEDGE GRAPH CONSTRUCTION

Purpose
Convert the extracted tuples into a graph structure.

Concept
A knowledge graph contains nodes and edges.

Nodes represent entities or values.
Edges represent relationships between them.

Example tuples

(Randle, born on, February 12 1949)
(Randle, played for, Seattle Mariners)

Graph nodes

Randle
February 12 1949
Seattle Mariners

Graph edges

Randle -- born on --> February 12 1949
Randle -- played for --> Seattle Mariners

Graph representation

Randle
├── born on → February 12 1949
└── played for → Seattle Mariners

The graph stores multiple facts about the same entity in a structured form.

STAGE 4: RELATION NORMALIZATION

Purpose
Convert the natural language relations extracted by OpenIE into standardized attribute names.

Problem
OpenIE relations appear in natural language and may vary.

Examples

played for
member of
signed with

All these phrases refer to the same concept: team membership.

Solution
Map similar relation phrases to standardized attribute names.

Example mapping

born on → birth_date
is → occupation
played for → team
member of → team
served as → position

Example transformation

Original tuple
(Randle, played for, Seattle Mariners)

Normalized tuple
(Randle, team, Seattle Mariners)

After normalization the tuples may look like

(Randle, birth_date, February 12 1949)
(Randle, occupation, baseball player)
(Randle, team, Seattle Mariners)

This produces a cleaner and more consistent knowledge graph.

STAGE 5: KNOWLEDGE GRAPH REFINEMENT

Purpose
Improve graph consistency and ensure all relations refer to the correct entity.

Tasks

1. Merge duplicate entities if they appear multiple times.
2. Perform coreference resolution to resolve pronouns.

Example paragraph

Randle debuted in 1971. He played for the Seattle Mariners.

Coreference resolution

He → Randle

Resulting tuples

(Randle, debut_year, 1971)
(Randle, team, Seattle Mariners)

Graph representation

Randle
├── debut_year → 1971
└── team → Seattle Mariners

This step ensures that information from multiple sentences is connected correctly in the graph.

STAGE 6: TABLE GENERATION FROM KNOWLEDGE GRAPH

Purpose
Convert the knowledge graph into a structured table.

Method
Each outgoing edge from the main person node becomes a row in the table.

Example graph

Randle
├── birth_date → February 12 1949
├── occupation → baseball player
└── team → Seattle Mariners

Converted table

attribute | value
birth_date | February 12 1949
occupation | baseball player
team | Seattle Mariners

The entity name becomes the main identifier.

Final table

name | Leonard Shenoff Randle
birth_date | February 12 1949
occupation | baseball player
team | Seattle Mariners

SUMMARY OF THE FULL PIPELINE

paragraph
↓
text preprocessing
↓
sentence segmentation
↓
OpenIE extraction
↓
knowledge graph construction
↓
relation normalization
↓
knowledge graph refinement
↓
table generation

The final system converts an unstructured paragraph into a structured table through an intermediate knowledge graph representation.
